Learn About Common Chronic Diseases

[Image of a human heart]

Heart Disease

Refers to a range of conditions that affect your heart. Early detection through risk factors like blood pressure and cholesterol is key to management.

Diabetes

A disease in which your blood glucose, or blood sugar, levels are too high. Monitoring glucose levels and BMI are crucial for prevention and control.

[Image of cells under a microscope]

Cancer

A disease in which some of the body’s cells grow uncontrollably and spread to other parts of the body. Lifestyle factors and biomarkers can indicate risk.

[Image of human joints]

Hypertension

Hypertension, also known as high blood pressure, is a chronic medical condition in which the force of blood against the artery walls remains consistently high. Over time, this increased pressure can cause serious health problems such as heart disease, stroke, and kidney damage. Often called the “silent killer”, hypertension may not show obvious symptoms until significant damage has occurred. That’s why early detection and monitoring are vital.
